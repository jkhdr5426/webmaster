How ProGlove Benefits Our Community

ProGlove’s wearable scanner system supports a wide range of local industries — including warehouses, retail backrooms, small manufacturers, and distribution centers. By replacing traditional handheld scanners with a lightweight, glove-based device, workers can scan items while keeping both hands free. This reduces physical strain, speeds up common tasks, and helps prevent repetitive-motion injuries.

Simple Pilot Plan

  1. Partner with a nearby warehouse, manufacturer, or retailer for a 2-week trial (10–20 participating workers).
  2. Obtain 5–10 ProGlove MARK scanners for testing, either through purchase or a vendor demo program.
  3. Record baseline data: scans per hour, pick accuracy, task completion times, and worker experience.
  4. Deploy the ProGlove units for 2 weeks, then compare productivity data and gather feedback from the team.

Estimated Costs

Exact pricing varies by model, but ProGlove scanners generally cost a few hundred dollars per device. Additional costs include charging stations, accessories, and brief user training. For a small-scale pilot, the expected budget ranges from $2,500–$6,000, depending on quantity and vendor discounts.

Real-World Factors to Keep in Mind

  • Connectivity: Devices connect via Bluetooth Low Energy or ProGlove’s proprietary radio, depending on the workstation setup.
  • Training & Adoption: Workers learn the device quickly. Reduced fatigue and faster scanning help encourage long-term use.
  • Maintenance: Establish a charging routine and keep a few backup scanners ready to ensure smooth daily operations.
