How this wearable helps our community

Primary beneficiaries: warehouse workers, retail stock teams, local manufacturers, and small distribution centers. Hands-free scanning reduces repetitive strain from handheld scanners and increases throughput by allowing workers to scan while carrying items.

Pilot plan (simple)

  1. Partner with a local warehouse or retailer for a 2-week pilot (10–20 workers).
  2. Procure 5–10 MARK devices as trial units (or request a vendor test/demo).
  3. Measure baseline: average scans per hour, error rate, and worker feedback.
  4. Deploy ProGlove devices for 2 weeks, then compare metrics and collect qualitative feedback.

Estimated costs (rough)

Device cost varies by model and vendor; expect several hundred dollars per unit (pricing and vendor discounts vary). Include incidental costs for chargers and training. A small pilot budget estimate: $2,500–$6,000 depending on number of units.

Real-world considerations

  • Connectivity: BLE or proprietary RF depending on terminal ecosystem.
  • Worker acceptance: short training, clear benefits (less fatigue) increase adoption.
  • Maintenance: battery charging schedule and spare units recommended.
